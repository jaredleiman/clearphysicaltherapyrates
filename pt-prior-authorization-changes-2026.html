Credentialing & Auth

PT Prior Authorization Changes in 2026: Which Carriers Tightened Requirements and What to Do

April 20268 min readClearPhysicalTherapyRates.com

Prior authorization requirements for physical therapy services have shifted significantly in 2026. Several major commercial carriers updated their thresholds, documentation requirements, and review processes — changes that took effect with minimal notice to in-network practices. Here's what changed and how to protect your revenue from authorization-related claim denials.

Why PA Requirements Are Getting More Complex

The prior authorization landscape for PT has been moving in two opposing directions simultaneously. Some carriers — particularly those participating in the Improving Seniors' Timely Access to Care Act compliance — have reduced certain PA requirements for Medicare Advantage PT services. At the same time, commercial plans (fully insured and self-funded) have been tightening PA requirements as a cost-control measure, adding steps and reducing initial authorization visit counts.

The result for PT practices: a more complex, carrier-specific authorization environment where last year's workflow may not match this year's requirements. Submitting claims without required authorizations — or with authorizations that have lapsed — is one of the most preventable causes of revenue cycle disruption in PT billing.

Medicare Advantage — PA Reform Updates

The Improving Seniors' Timely Access to Care Act requires Medicare Advantage plans to reduce unnecessary prior authorization for PT services. In 2026, CMS published additional guidance on implementation expectations, and several major MA plans updated their PA policies for PT:

Medicare Advantage — General Update

CMS reiterated in 2026 guidance that MA plans cannot require prior authorization for PT services that would be covered without PA under traditional Medicare. For PT services that are standard of care and clearly medically necessary, blanket PA requirements may be inconsistent with CMS guidance.

If your MA plan PA denials are for services that are routine and clearly covered under traditional Medicare, the CMS guidance provides grounds for appeal. Document the medical necessity clearly and reference the 2024 Act compliance requirements in your appeal.

UnitedHealth Commercial — PA Threshold Changes

UnitedHealth / UHC Commercial

Initial Authorization Visits (selected markets): UHC updated its initial authorization visit allocation for musculoskeletal PT in several regional markets. In affected markets, initial authorizations were reduced from 12 visits to 8 visits, requiring earlier concurrent review requests to avoid authorization lapses between approval periods.

Functional Outcome Requirement: UHC now requires a functional outcome measure to be submitted with concurrent review requests for authorizations beyond the initial allocation. The specific measure is not mandated, but must be a validated tool (FOTO, LEFS, DASH, PSFS, or equivalent) with a baseline and current measurement.

Action: If you have UHC commercial patients in the initial 8-visit authorization period, initiate concurrent review at visit 5 or 6 — not at visit 8. Late requests risk authorization gaps.

Aetna — Concurrent Review Documentation Update

Aetna Commercial

Concurrent Review Submission Requirements: Aetna updated its concurrent review submission requirements for PT authorizations. Beginning Q1 2026, concurrent review requests must include: (1) the specific functional outcome measure used at the start of care and the current measurement, (2) the remaining functional goals and estimated visits to achieve them, and (3) a brief statement of why continued skilled PT is necessary rather than a home exercise program.

Submission Method: Aetna has migrated most PT authorization requests to its Availity portal. Phone-based authorization requests for PT are no longer accepted in most markets as of 2026.

Cigna — Active Care Path and Authorization

Cigna

Active Care Path Integration: Cigna's active care path requirements are now integrated with its authorization process for musculoskeletal PT. Practices that are not following the Cigna active care path protocol for covered conditions may face authorization delays or denials on concurrent review requests.

Visit Thresholds: Cigna maintained its general visit thresholds for initial PT authorizations but tightened concurrent review documentation requirements. For episodes exceeding 16 visits, Cigna requires a physician attestation of medical necessity in addition to the PT's clinical documentation.

Building a PA Compliance Workflow

The single most effective way to prevent authorization-related claim denials is a documented, consistently followed authorization tracking workflow. Key elements:

  • Verify PA requirements at intake — every patient, every payer. PA requirements change. A patient whose plan didn't require PA last year may require it now. Verify current requirements at each episode of care, not just for new patients.
  • Track visit counts against authorization limits. Set an alert in your practice management system when patients reach 60% of their authorized visits. This gives you time to initiate concurrent review before the authorization expires.
  • Document functional outcomes from day one. Multiple carriers now require functional outcome measurements for concurrent review. If you wait until the concurrent review is due to take the measurement, you don't have a baseline to compare against. Measure at intake, measure at every 5–6 visits, and measure at discharge.
  • Know each carrier's submission method. Aetna went portal-only. Some carriers still require phone requests. Using the wrong method delays authorization and delays care.
Revenue Cycle Impact

Authorization lapses — treating patients between the expiration of one authorization and approval of the next — are one of the leading causes of PT claim denials. Most carriers will not retroactively authorize services provided during an authorization gap, even when the services are clearly medically necessary. Proactive tracking is the only protection.

PA requirements and policies described reflect publicly available carrier guidance as of April 2026. Requirements vary by market, plan type, and individual provider contract. Always verify current PA requirements directly with each carrier through your provider portal or contracting representative.
