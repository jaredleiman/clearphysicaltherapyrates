Medicare Part B

Medicare PFS 2026: What the Conversion Factor Cut Means for Your PT Practice Revenue

April 20268 min readClearPhysicalTherapyRates.com

The 2026 Medicare Physician Fee Schedule took effect January 1 with a 2.8% conversion factor reduction — the largest single-year cut in over a decade. For a PT practice billing $500,000 annually to Medicare, that's approximately $14,000 in reduced reimbursement before any other adjustments.

Understanding the Conversion Factor

Every Medicare CPT code is valued in Relative Value Units — a measure of the physician work, practice expense, and malpractice overhead associated with the service. The conversion factor is the dollar multiplier applied to those RVUs to produce the Medicare allowable amount.

When CMS reduces the conversion factor, every Medicare-covered service you bill is affected proportionally. A 2.8% reduction in the conversion factor produces approximately a 2.8% reduction in the Medicare allowable for each CPT code — unless the code's underlying RVU value was also changed.

The 2026 conversion factor is $32.35, down from $33.29 in 2025.

The Revenue Math for a Typical PT Practice

To understand the real-dollar impact, consider a single-location PT practice with the following Medicare billing profile:

  • 40 Medicare visits per week, averaging 3 units of 97110 and 2 units of 97530 per visit
  • Annual Medicare billing: approximately $520,000
  • Medicare as percentage of payer mix: 35%

At a 2.8% conversion factor reduction, this practice sees approximately $14,560 in reduced annual Medicare reimbursement — without any change in patient volume or service intensity. That's the equivalent of losing roughly 3 weeks of Medicare revenue per year.

Action Required

Review your Medicare allowable rates in your practice management software. If your software stores hard-coded rates rather than calculating from the current conversion factor, you may be billing on 2025 rates — which affects your posted charges and any balance billing compliance requirements.

What Drove the 2026 Reduction

The conversion factor reduction is driven by two statutory mechanisms. First, the Medicare Access and CHIP Reauthorization Act (MACRA) ties conversion factor updates to a formula that has produced flat or negative adjustments in recent years. Second, CMS applies budget neutrality adjustments when it increases RVU values for some services — required by law to offset those increases by reducing the conversion factor.

In 2026, CMS increased RVU values for several evaluation and management codes and primary care services. The budget neutrality adjustment required to offset those increases drove a larger-than-average conversion factor cut — disproportionately affecting therapy services, which didn't receive corresponding RVU increases.

What Changed Beyond the Conversion Factor

Several CPT-level changes in the 2026 PFS also affect PT practices beyond the conversion factor reduction:

Remote Therapeutic Monitoring Codes

CMS clarified the documentation and supervision requirements for RTM codes (98975, 98976, 98977, 98980, 98981) in the 2026 final rule. PT practices billing RTM codes for musculoskeletal conditions should review the updated guidance on general supervision requirements and the 16-day minimum data collection threshold.

Telehealth PT — Extended Through Year-End

Medicare telehealth coverage for PT evaluation and management services has been extended through December 31, 2026 under existing waiver authority. Documentation requirements for telehealth visits differ from in-person visits — specifically around technology platform documentation and patient consent.

KX Modifier Threshold Update

The 2026 therapy cap exception threshold — the amount at which the KX modifier is required — has been updated. PT practices must apply the KX modifier to Medicare claims once a beneficiary's cumulative physical and speech therapy costs exceed the threshold for the year.

Three Actions Before Your Next Billing Cycle

  1. Update posted charge amounts. If your practice posts charges as a percentage markup over Medicare allowable, the 2026 conversion factor change affects your posted charges. Update your charge master before the next billing run.
  2. Review your payer mix revenue projections. If you built 2026 revenue projections using 2025 Medicare rates, reforecast using the updated allowables for your highest-volume codes.
  3. Check your practice management software update schedule. Most PM systems update Medicare fee schedules automatically, but confirm the update has been applied and verify rates for your 5 most-billed CPT codes against the 2026 allowables.
