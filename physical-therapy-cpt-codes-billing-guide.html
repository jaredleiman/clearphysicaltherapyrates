Physical Therapy CPT Codes: Complete Billing Reference

By ClearPhysicalTherapyRates · April 2026 · 10 min read

Physical therapy billing uses dozens of CPT codes across several categories — evaluations, therapeutic interventions, modalities, and tests. Selecting the correct code for each service determines reimbursement accuracy and audit risk. This guide covers every commonly used PT code with definitions and billing rules.

Evaluation Codes (97161-97164)

CodeDescriptionComplexityTypical Time
97161PT evaluation, low complexity1-2 body regions, mild problem20-30 min
97162PT evaluation, moderate complexity2-3 body regions, moderate problem30-45 min
97163PT evaluation, high complexity3+ body regions, complex problem45-60 min
97164PT re-evaluationAny complexity change20-30 min

Evaluation complexity is determined by three factors: the patient's history and presenting problem, the number of body systems examined, and the clinical decision-making required. A patient with isolated knee pain after meniscus surgery is typically 97161 (low). A patient with multiple joint involvement, comorbidities, and complex functional limitations is 97163 (high).

Therapeutic Procedure Codes (Timed)

CodeDescriptionKey Detail
97110Therapeutic exerciseStrength, ROM, endurance, flexibility exercises
97112Neuromuscular re-educationBalance, coordination, posture, proprioception, motor control
97116Gait trainingWalking-specific training including assistive device use
97140Manual therapyJoint mobilization, soft tissue mobilization, manual traction
97530Therapeutic activitiesFunctional, dynamic activities simulating real-life tasks
97535Self-care/home management trainingADL training, compensatory technique instruction
97542Wheelchair managementWheelchair mobility, transfers, propulsion training
97750Physical performance testFunctional capacity evaluations, standardized testing

All therapeutic procedure codes are timed in 15-minute units using the 8-minute rule. They require direct one-on-one patient contact — the therapist must be present and actively involved during the entire billed time.

Modality Codes (Supervised and Constant Attendance)

CodeDescriptionType
97010Hot/cold packsSupervised (untimed)
97012Mechanical tractionSupervised (untimed)
97014Electrical stimulation (unattended)Supervised (untimed)
97032Electrical stimulation (attended)Constant attendance (timed)
97035UltrasoundConstant attendance (timed)
97033IontophoresisConstant attendance (timed)

Supervised modalities (untimed) are billed as a single unit regardless of how long they're applied. Constant attendance modalities (timed) are billed in 15-minute units and require the therapist to be present the entire time. Key distinction: supervised modalities do not count toward the total timed minutes when calculating unit allocation across multiple timed codes.

Group Therapy

97150 (Group therapy) — Therapeutic procedures performed with 2 or more patients simultaneously. Billed per patient, not per group. The therapist must provide intermittent direct contact to each patient during the session. Group therapy is billed as a single unit per session regardless of duration. It cannot be billed alongside individual timed codes for overlapping time periods.

The 8-Minute Rule and Unit Calculation

When multiple timed codes are used in the same session, add up all timed minutes, determine the total number of billable units, and allocate units to the codes that received the most minutes. The minimum for any billable unit is 8 minutes. Remaining minutes that don't reach the 8-minute threshold for any code are not billable.

Example: A session includes 20 min of 97110, 15 min of 97140, and 10 min of 97530. Total: 45 min = 3 billable units. Allocation: 97110 gets 1 unit (20 min), 97140 gets 1 unit (15 min), 97530 gets 1 unit (10 min — exceeds the 8-minute threshold).

The Bottom Line

Accurate CPT code selection is the foundation of PT billing. Each code has specific definitions, documentation requirements, and billing rules. The most common errors — confusing 97110 with 97530, miscounting timed units, and billing supervised modalities as timed codes — are preventable with clear understanding of each code's scope. When in doubt, reference the CPT manual definition and document specifically enough that an auditor can see exactly which code applies.
