Does Medicaid Cover Physical Therapy?

By ClearPhysicalTherapyRates · April 2026 · 7 min read

Yes — Medicaid covers physical therapy, though the specifics vary significantly by state. For children under 21, coverage is guaranteed through the federal EPSDT mandate. For adults, coverage is an optional benefit that each state chooses whether and how to provide. Here's how Medicaid PT coverage works across both populations.

Children: Guaranteed Coverage Under EPSDT

For Medicaid-eligible children and young adults under 21, physical therapy is covered in every state through the Early and Periodic Screening, Diagnostic, and Treatment (EPSDT) mandate. EPSDT requires state Medicaid programs to cover any medically necessary treatment — including physical therapy — regardless of whether the state's Medicaid plan lists PT as a covered benefit for adults.

Under EPSDT, there are no arbitrary visit limits — coverage is based on medical necessity. If a child needs 60 visits of PT per year for a qualifying condition, Medicaid must cover all 60 visits if they are medically necessary. States cannot impose caps that restrict medically necessary treatment for EPSDT-eligible beneficiaries.

Adults: Coverage Varies by State

For adults, physical therapy is classified as an optional Medicaid benefit under federal law. This means each state decides whether to include PT in its Medicaid program, how many visits to cover, whether prior authorization is required, and what conditions qualify.

As of 2026, the majority of states include some level of adult PT coverage in their Medicaid programs. However, common limitations include annual visit caps (often 20-30 visits per year), prior authorization requirements after an initial set of visits, requirement for a physician referral, and lower reimbursement rates compared to Medicare and commercial insurance (often 30-50% lower).

A few states have very limited or no outpatient PT coverage for adults on Medicaid. In these states, PT may only be covered in inpatient or home health settings.

Medicaid Managed Care

Most states deliver Medicaid through managed care organizations (MCOs) rather than traditional fee-for-service. Each MCO may have its own PT coverage rules, provider networks, and prior authorization processes — even within the same state. If you have Medicaid managed care, contact your specific MCO to verify PT coverage, find in-network providers, and understand any authorization requirements.

What Patients Typically Pay

Medicaid patients generally pay little to no cost for physical therapy. Most states charge no copay for PT services. A few states impose nominal copays of $1-$5 per visit. Copays cannot be charged for EPSDT services for children. Medicaid providers cannot bill patients for amounts above the Medicaid-allowed rate.

Access challenge: While Medicaid covers PT, finding a provider who accepts Medicaid can be difficult. Low reimbursement rates mean many PT practices limit the number of Medicaid patients they accept or don't accept Medicaid at all. Hospital-based outpatient departments and community health centers are more likely to accept Medicaid than private PT practices.

How to Access PT Through Medicaid

  1. Check your coverage — Contact your Medicaid plan or MCO to confirm PT is covered and understand any visit limits or authorization requirements
  2. Get a referral if required — Some states and MCOs require a physician referral for PT. Others allow direct access.
  3. Find an in-network provider — Use your MCO's provider directory or call practices in your area to ask if they accept your specific Medicaid plan
  4. Request prior authorization if needed — Your PT provider typically handles this, but confirm that authorization has been obtained before starting treatment

For Providers: Medicaid PT Considerations

Providers considering accepting Medicaid PT patients should understand that reimbursement rates are typically 30-50% below Medicare rates, prior authorization and documentation requirements are extensive, claims processing and payment timelines are often slower than commercial payers, and audit and compliance requirements are rigorous. Despite these challenges, serving Medicaid patients fills a critical access gap and can contribute to practice volume, community reputation, and mission-driven care.

The Bottom Line

Medicaid covers physical therapy for children in every state and for adults in most states. The coverage is real, but the details — visit limits, authorization requirements, and provider availability — vary significantly by state and by specific Medicaid plan. For patients, the most important step is contacting your plan to confirm coverage and find in-network providers. For providers, the decision to accept Medicaid is a business calculation that balances lower reimbursement against community impact and volume.
