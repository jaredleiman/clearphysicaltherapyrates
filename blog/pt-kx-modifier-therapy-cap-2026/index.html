Medicare Compliance

The KX Modifier and Therapy Cap Exceptions: What PT Practices Must Document in 2026

April 20267 min readClearPhysicalTherapyRates.com

Medicare covers PT services beyond the annual therapy cap only when the KX modifier is applied — and only when your documentation demonstrates that the care is medically necessary and the patient is making measurable progress. Here's exactly what's required in 2026 and what the updated threshold means for your practice.

The 2026 Therapy Cap Thresholds

$2,410 Combined PT + Speech Therapy threshold for 2026
$2,410 Occupational Therapy separate threshold for 2026

The therapy cap thresholds are updated annually based on the Medicare Economic Index. In 2026, the combined PT and speech-language pathology threshold is $2,410 — up slightly from the 2025 threshold. Once a Medicare beneficiary's cumulative PT and SLP costs for the calendar year exceed this amount, the KX modifier is required on every subsequent claim to assert medical necessity for continued care.

Occupational therapy has its own separate threshold of $2,410 and does not count against the PT/SLP threshold.

What the KX Modifier Actually Asserts

When you append modifier KX to a therapy claim, you are making a formal representation to Medicare that:

  1. The services are medically necessary
  2. The patient continues to benefit from skilled PT intervention
  3. Documentation in the medical record supports continued skilled care
  4. The patient has not reached maximum therapeutic benefit

This is not a routine modifier that can be applied mechanically once the threshold is crossed. Applying KX without supporting documentation is a false claim. Your medical record must contain contemporary evidence that supports each element of the assertion.

Documentation Requirements for KX Modifier Claims

Medicare contractors audit KX modifier claims at higher rates than pre-threshold claims, specifically looking for documentation that supports the medical necessity assertion. Your chart must contain:

Functional Progress Documentation

At a minimum, your documentation should include objective functional measures that demonstrate the patient is making progress toward established functional goals. Acceptable measures include standardized outcome tools (FOTO, LEFS, DASH, PSFS, BERG) and documented functional status compared to baseline. "Patient continues to improve" without objective measurement is insufficient to support KX modifier claims.

Skilled Care Justification

Post-threshold claims require clear documentation of why the patient's condition continues to require the skills of a licensed PT rather than a maintenance program the patient could perform independently or with a caregiver. The complexity of the treatment, the need for ongoing assessment and modification, and the patient's inability to perform the program without skilled supervision must be evident from the documentation.

Goal Documentation

Your plan of care goals should be updated to reflect the patient's current status and remaining functional deficits. Goals that were met earlier in the episode should be marked as achieved; new goals should reflect the specific functional outcomes being targeted in the continued treatment phase.

Audit Risk Alert

KX modifier claims are a consistent target in Medicare post-payment reviews. Auditors request medical records for claims where the KX modifier was applied and assess whether the documentation supports the medical necessity assertion. The most common failure: functional outcome measurements are absent or not updated at the frequency required to demonstrate ongoing progress.

Medical Review Thresholds

Beyond the KX modifier threshold, Medicare has an automatic Medical Review (MR) threshold — a higher dollar amount at which claims are subject to additional scrutiny even with the KX modifier applied. The 2026 automatic medical review threshold is:

  • $3,000 — combined PT and SLP services
  • $3,000 — OT services (separate threshold)

When cumulative costs exceed the MR threshold, Medicare contractors may request records for review before paying claims. This is not an audit — it's a pre-payment review process. Having complete, contemporaneous documentation for all KX modifier claims is the only protection against pre-payment review denials.

Tracking Patient Accumulation Toward the Threshold

Most practice management systems can be configured to track cumulative Medicare therapy costs per beneficiary for the calendar year. If your system doesn't automatically flag when patients approach the KX threshold, establish a manual tracking process — weekly review of Medicare patients with significant PT history in the current year.

Applying KX too early (before the threshold is actually crossed) does not create liability but may trigger unnecessary scrutiny. Failing to apply KX after the threshold is crossed means claims will be denied. Build in a buffer — begin tracking patients carefully at $2,000 cumulative costs and verify threshold status before submitting any claim for a patient in that range.
