Commercial Carriers

UnitedHealth, Aetna, and Cigna PT Rate Changes in 2026: What Every Practice Needs to Know

April 20269 min readClearPhysicalTherapyRates.com

Commercial carriers — UnitedHealth, Aetna, Cigna, BCBS, Humana — update their PT fee schedules on independent calendars with minimal advance notice to practices. Most practices discover rate changes when they check an EOB months after the revision took effect. Here's what changed in 2026 and how to stay ahead of future updates.

Why Commercial Rate Monitoring Is Harder Than Medicare

Medicare fee schedule changes are published in the Federal Register and take effect January 1 each year — predictable, centralized, publicly available. Commercial carrier rate changes are different in every way.

Each carrier publishes fee schedule revisions on its own schedule. Some carriers update rates annually in January; others update regionally on quarterly schedules; some update specific CPT code categories independently of the broader fee schedule. There is no central notification system. Carriers are not required to notify in-network providers before implementing fee schedule changes — though most provider agreements include some notice period.

The practical result: a Cigna fee schedule revision for 97110 in Ohio may take effect in March while the same code in Texas isn't revised until September. A practice with locations in both states needs to track two different effective dates for the same code with the same carrier.

UnitedHealth PT Rate Changes — 2026

UnitedHealth / UHC

Active Care Path Policy Update (Q1 2026): UHC revised its Active Care Path documentation requirements for PT services. Claims for treatment sessions beyond the initial evaluation now require documented functional outcome measures at each visit. The specific measure requirement varies by condition category — musculoskeletal conditions require at least one validated functional outcome tool (FOTO, LEFS, DASH, or equivalent).

Prior Authorization Threshold Changes: UHC increased the number of PT visits that can be authorized without concurrent review in several regional markets. Check your specific UHC contract supplement for your market.

Fee Schedule Revision Cycle: UHC typically revises PT fee schedules in Q1 for the new contract year. Practices with UHC contracts should verify current rates directly in the UHC provider portal for their specific contract.

Aetna PT Rate and Policy Changes — 2026

Aetna

97530 Clinical Policy Bulletin Update (May 2026): Aetna revised its clinical policy bulletin for therapeutic activities (97530). The updated policy requires documented functional limitation in the same visit note as the 97530 service — a notation of "therapeutic activities performed" without a linked functional limitation is no longer sufficient for claim support. Update your documentation templates before your next billing cycle.

State-Level Rate Revisions: Aetna implemented regional fee schedule adjustments for PT services in several southeastern states effective Q2 2026. Practices in Georgia, Tennessee, and South Carolina should verify current contracted rates.

Credentialing Window: Aetna's PT credentialing window for new in-network applications is open through Q3 2026 in several markets where network access gaps exist.

Cigna PT Coverage Policy Changes — 2026

Cigna

Active Care Path Requirements: Cigna updated its coverage policy for musculoskeletal PT services to require documented active care path compliance. For episodes extending beyond 12 visits, Cigna now requires a documented functional outcome measure and a clinical rationale for continued skilled PT.

Bundling Policy Change — 97110 + 97530: Cigna clarified its bundling policy for 97110 and 97530 billed on the same date of service. Both codes can be billed together only when the documentation clearly delineates the time spent on each service and establishes clinical rationale for both. Generic documentation of "exercise and functional activities" without specificity will result in bundling to the higher-paid code.

BCBS and Humana — Key Updates

Blue Cross Blue Shield plan updates vary significantly by state plan. National BCBS has provided guidance that individual state plans should be implementing outcome measure requirements for PT episodes exceeding 10 visits — but implementation timing varies by state plan. Check with your specific state BCBS plan for current requirements.

Humana updated its Remote Therapeutic Monitoring bundle policy effective May 2026. For PT practices billing RTM codes for musculoskeletal conditions under Humana MA plans, the updated policy requires a minimum of 16 days of monitoring data per 30-day period and documentation of patient engagement with the monitoring program.

The Monitoring Problem

Most PT practices have no systematic process for detecting carrier fee schedule changes. The typical discovery path: a billing manager notices an EOB with a lower allowed amount, investigates, and discovers the carrier revised the fee schedule 3 months ago. By then, 90 days of claims have been submitted and paid at the lower rate — with no recourse. Proactive monitoring is the only way to avoid this.

How to Track Commercial Carrier Rate Changes

There is no single source for all commercial carrier rate changes. The most reliable monitoring approach combines multiple sources:

  • Provider portal alerts: Log into each carrier's provider portal and set up email alerts for policy and fee schedule updates. Most major carriers have a notification preference setting.
  • Contract review: Re-read your provider agreement's notice provisions. Most contracts require 30–90 days advance notice of fee schedule changes. If you're not receiving notices, you may not be registered correctly with the carrier.
  • Regular EOB auditing: Spot-check EOBs for your 5 highest-volume CPT codes against your current fee schedule quarterly. A discrepancy is usually the first signal of a rate change.
  • APTA and state chapter resources: APTA monitors major carrier policy changes affecting PT and publishes alerts through its member communications.

Commercial carrier rate and policy information reflects publicly available guidance as of April 2026. Contracted rates vary by practice and agreement. Always verify current rates and policies directly with each carrier through your provider portal or contracting representative.
